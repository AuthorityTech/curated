## What is LLM SEO?

LLM SEO is the practice of optimizing content so that large language models, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and similar AI systems, can understand, cite, and surface it in their responses.

Unlike traditional SEO, which focuses on ranking in search results, LLM SEO focuses on citations. AI engines don't show ten blue links. They generate synthesized answers and cite sources inline. If you're not part of that synthesized answer, you're invisible.

The shift is significant:

  - **Traditional SEO:** Optimize for rankings, measure position and clicks

  - **LLM SEO:** Optimize for citations, measure mentions in AI responses

Why does this matter now? According to Semrush research, AI search visitors convert 4.4x better than traditional organic visitors. As AI search adoption accelerates, with 27% of US users now preferring AI tools over traditional search, LLM SEO becomes essential for visibility.

## The Problem with Most LLM SEO Advice

Search for LLM SEO strategies and you'll find the same recommendations everywhere:

  - Structure content in chunks (100-300 tokens)

  - Optimize for entities, not just keywords

  - Use semantic formatting and clear definitions

  - Include structured data and schema markup

These tactics help, and you should implement them. But they miss the fundamental question: **what sources do AI engines actually cite?**

LLMs don't cite sources equally. They prioritize certain source types over others based on patterns in their training data and real-time retrieval systems. Understanding this hierarchy is the key to effective LLM SEO.

## How LLMs Decide What to Cite

Understanding the citation hierarchy helps you prioritize your LLM SEO efforts:

### High Citation Priority

  - Forbes, TechCrunch, Wall Street Journal, major news outlets

  - Industry-specific authoritative publications

  - Wikipedia and established reference sources

  - Academic papers and research institutions

### Medium Citation Priority

  - Well-established industry blogs with authority

  - Professional association content

  - Government and institutional sources

### Low Citation Priority

  - Brand websites and corporate blogs

  - Product pages and promotional content

  - User-generated content without clear authority

When ChatGPT or Perplexity synthesizes an answer, they pull from high-priority sources first. Your perfectly optimized website competes against Forbes articles, and Forbes wins nearly every time.

## The Two Paths to LLM Visibility

You have two options for LLM SEO:

### Path 1: On-Page Optimization (Necessary But Insufficient)

On-page optimization improves how LLMs understand your content. Implement these tactics:

  - Structure content in logical chunks with clear headers

  - Define terms explicitly for AI extraction

  - Include data points and statistics LLMs can cite

  - Use schema markup and structured data

  - Optimize for conversational queries

These tactics are necessary. They make your content LLM-friendly. But they don't solve the source hierarchy problem. Even perfectly optimized brand content loses to third-party publications.

Earned media bypasses the source hierarchy problem entirely. When Forbes publishes an article featuring your company, you inherit Forbes' authority in AI systems.
