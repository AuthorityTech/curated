## The Retainer Problem: Why 70% of Clients Churn

Data reveals three fundamental flaws that make PR retainers a trap for brands seeking real media coverage.

### 70% Churn Rate Within 6 Months

Research shows that 70% of PR retainer clients exit within 6 months, frustrated by vague metrics, lack of transparency, and no guaranteed results. Traditional retainers operate on a model that benefits agencies, not brands: agencies get paid monthly regardless of outcomes, creating misaligned incentives.

When agencies can't guarantee placements, they focus on process over results. They deliver strategy documents, outreach reports, and monitoring dashboards, but no actual media coverage. This creates a fundamental disconnect: brands pay for PR, but get project management instead of placements.

### 68% Lack Placement Metrics

68% of PR retainers lack placement metrics, making it impossible to measure ROI or track success. Without clear metrics, brands can't determine if their PR investment is working. They're paying $5,000+ per month, but have no way to verify if they're getting value.

This lack of transparency is intentional. When agencies can't guarantee placements, they avoid metrics that would expose their failure. Instead, they report on activities: emails sent, meetings held, strategies developed. But activity doesn't equal results.

### $5,000+ Monthly Costs with No Guarantees

Traditional retainers charge $5,000+ per month (the low end), with enterprise clients paying $50,000 to $100,000+ monthly. These fees are charged regardless of results: you pay for strategy, outreach, and monitoring, but get no placement guarantees.

This creates a fundamental misalignment: agencies get paid whether they secure placements or not. There's no urgency to deliver results, no accountability for outcomes, and no incentive to optimize for performance. Brands pay retainers hoping for placements, but agencies get paid regardless.

Guaranteed PR placements flip the traditional retainer model entirely. Instead of charging monthly fees regardless of results, agencies only get paid when they secure actual media coverage.

### Earned Media vs. Paid Content

Guaranteed PR placements deliver earned media, not paid content. Earned media is editorial coverage secured through legitimate PR work: journalists write about your brand because it's newsworthy, not because you paid for it.

This is different from paid content or sponsored posts, which are advertisements disguised as editorial. Earned media has credibility because it's written by independent journalists who chose to cover your story. Paid content lacks this credibility because readers know it's an advertisement.

Guaranteed PR placement agencies secure earned media in Tier 1 publications like Forbes, TechCrunch, and The Wall Street Journal. These are legitimate editorial placements, not paid advertisements.
