## The Core Flaws of PR Retainers

### No ROI Guarantees

According to LinkedIn analysis, 68% of PR retainers lack placement metrics. This means agencies charge monthly fees without tracking or guaranteeing actual media coverage. Clients pay $5,000+ per month for emails, strategy calls, and monitoring reports, but no placements.

The retainer model creates a fundamental misalignment: agencies get paid regardless of results. There's no incentive to secure placements when the monthly check arrives either way. This leads to what industry insiders call "retainer drift", agencies start strong but gradually reduce effort as the relationship stabilizes.

### Churn Epidemic

Data shows that 70% of PR retainer clients exit within 6 months. Reddit threads and industry forums are filled with brands citing "ghosted pitches," "junior work billed as senior time," and "no results after months of retainers."

This churn rate is catastrophic for agencies, but it's even worse for brands. They waste months and thousands of dollars before realizing the retainer isn't delivering. By the time they switch, they've lost momentum, budget, and trust in PR as a channel.

The churn isn't just about poor results, it's about the fundamental misalignment of the retainer model. When agencies get paid regardless of outcomes, quality inevitably declines.

### Opaque Hours and Billing

Retainer agreements promise "senior executive time" but often deliver junior coordinator work. Agencies bill for "strategy sessions" that are actually status updates, "outreach" that's automated email blasts, and "monitoring" that's basic Google Alerts.

Brands have no visibility into how their retainer dollars are actually spent. Is that $5,000 monthly fee going toward securing TechCrunch coverage, or is it funding internal agency overhead? The retainer model makes it impossible to know.
