**Your pitch didn't reach a human.** It was filtered out by an algorithm that decided—in milliseconds—that your message wasn't relevant, your source wasn't credible, or your angle didn't match the patterns journalists are searching for.

Welcome to machine relations.

Gab Ferree, founder of Off the Record, coined this term during an [Axios HQ webinar in early February 2026](https://stacker.com/blog/media-relations-are-becoming-machine-relations-and-most-brands-arent-ready), and it instantly crystallized what PR professionals have been experiencing for the past 18 months: **the relationship between brands and media coverage has fundamentally changed because journalists now rely on AI systems to decide what deserves their attention.**

Read the full article: [Machine Relations: How AI Newsrooms Are Filtering Your Pitches](https://authoritytech.io/blog/machine-relations-ai-newsrooms-filtering-pitches-2026)

## Key Insights

- **96% of publishers** now prioritize AI for back-end automation, including pitch filtering
- **59% of PR practitioners** say AI will grow in importance over the next five years
- **45% of PR professionals** have seen AI create something that puts their brand at risk
- Machine relations introduces a third party: AI systems that filter before journalists see your pitch
- Your owned content quality is now part of source validation—journalists use AI to vet you

## What This Means

The shift to AI-filtered pitches isn't a preference—it's a necessity driven by newsroom economics and information overload. If your pitch doesn't contain machine-readable signals that match what AI systems recognize as newsworthy, you're invisible to the algorithmic gatekeeper that controls access to journalists.
